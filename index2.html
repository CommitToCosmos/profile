About Me

I am a highly motivated researcher and educator with a strong background in Astronomy and Astrophysics, particularly in radio astronomy. My work focuses on investigating extragalactic compact radio sources[cite: 42, 44]. I have experience as an Assistant Professor, teaching subjects like Scientific Data Analysis with Python and Computational Physics[cite: 13, 16]. I am passionate about making science accessible and am a founding member of the Syzygy astronomy and public outreach initiative.

Research Experience

Extragalactic Compact Radio Sources [cite: 42]

Supervisors: Prof. Dhruba J. Saikia (IUCAA, Pune), Dr. Yogesh Chandola (Purple Mountain Observatory, CAS, China) [cite: 43]

My work focuses on investigating asymmetries in Compact Steep Spectrum (CSS) and Peaked Spectrum (PS) sources[cite: 44]. These are likely young radio galaxies, and by using HI column densities and symmetry parameters, we investigate whether their observed shapes are due to orientation or their interaction with a dense environment[cite: 45, 46].

Pulsar Magnetic Inclination Angle [cite: 47]

Supervisor: Prof. Sushan Konar (NCRA-TIFR, Pune) [cite: 48]

Conducted an exploratory analysis on how the magnetic inclination angle impacts pulsar emission properties by examining correlations between the angle and pulse profile shapes[cite: 48, 49].

Radio Image Analysis Using CASA [cite: 50]

Supervisor: Prof. K.S. Dwarakanath (Raman Research Institute, Bangalore) [cite: 51]

Gained experience in radio image synthesis, including calibration and deconvolution[cite: 51]. I processed raw interferometer data from the Very Large Array (VLA) and ALMA, creating HI radio images of galaxy NGC 5921 and a sub-mm continuum image of TW Hydra[cite: 52, 53, 54].
